# Evaluation Framework

## Purpose
This document defines the standard evaluation methodology used by the IT team to assess partner responses to RFPs. It ensures fair, consistent, and transparent evaluation across all procurement engagements.

---

## Scoring Scale

| Score | Rating | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Exceeds requirements significantly. Demonstrated evidence of superior capability. Innovative approach that adds measurable value. |
| 4 | Good | Fully meets all requirements. Clear evidence provided. Well-structured and comprehensive response. |
| 3 | Acceptable | Meets minimum requirements. Adequate evidence provided. Some areas could be stronger. |
| 2 | Below Expectations | Partially meets requirements. Gaps identified. Limited evidence or vague commitments. |
| 1 | Poor | Does not meet requirements. Significant gaps. Insufficient evidence or understanding. |
| 0 | Not Addressed | Requirement not covered in the response at all. |

## Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Individual Scoring
Each evaluator independently scores every partner response against all criteria using the scoring scale above. Each score must include a written justification (1-2 sentences).

### Step 2: Consensus Meeting
Evaluators meet to discuss scores where there is significant variance (difference > 2 points). Agreement is reached through discussion, not averaging.

### Step 3: Weighted Score Calculation
```
Weighted Score = Σ (Criterion Score × Criterion Weight)
```
Maximum possible weighted score = 5.0

### Step 4: Compliance Verification
Any partner scoring 0 on a Must Have requirement is flagged as non-compliant regardless of overall score.

### Step 5: Commercial Normalisation
Commercial proposals are normalised to enable fair comparison:
- Convert to same currency using the exchange rate on RFP issuance date
- Normalise to same duration/scope
- Identify and quantify hidden costs or exclusions

### Step 6: Final Ranking
Partners are ranked by weighted score. The recommendation considers:
1. Weighted score ranking
2. Compliance status
3. Risk assessment
4. Commercial normalisation results
5. Reference check outcomes

---

## Conflict of Interest
All evaluators must declare any potential conflicts of interest before participating in the evaluation. Any evaluator with a conflict must recuse themselves from scoring that partner's response.

---

## Record Keeping
All evaluation scoresheets, meeting notes, and the final report must be retained in the designated SharePoint library for a minimum of 3 years for audit purposes.
